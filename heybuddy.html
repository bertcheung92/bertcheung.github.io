Hey Buddy

A 5-minute short film about a working person who reconnects with a special someone.
  • Bert Cheung
  • Daniella Valdes
  • Michelle Swolfs
  • Max Proske
My Roles: Director of Photography, Camerawork, Writer, Editor

Process

As part of our videography class we were to make a 5-minute shortfilm based on anything we wanted. We brainstormed together and decided we wanted to tell a story of loneliness and the workplace. We asked the West Vancouver Memorial Library for filming permission and we shot all of our footage there and at the beachside nearby with two DSLR cameras and tripods. Audio was recorded using a boom mic and lapel microphones.

Our biggest hurdle came when we were recording audio. We did not have the audio recorder set on the right input for the boom mic, so all of our audio came straight from the recording unit and not the boom mic! We had to rerecord our audio footage and did ADR in the back of the car the next day, as well as finish reshoots and shooting the ending.

I was involved with postproduction. I edited the raw footage together and did audio and foley with Adobe Premiere Pro and the final render.

Result

I learned so much from the filming process for the short film! The biggest mistakes we made was not checking the audio recorder since that costed us a lot of time and effort. It was a fun experience getting to know how things go when making a film from start to finish.

Some of the skills I've used include:

  • Writing the story structure and some dialogue
  • Using theory learned in previous photography courses to set up lighting and using natural lighting to its advantage
  • Shooting footage and coordinating camera shots
  • Organizing and editing raw footage together
  • Working in a strict deadline as we only had weeks from preproduction to post production.
  • Post-production work including sound mixing, foley, and rendering
