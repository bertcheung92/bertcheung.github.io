Into the Paintscape

Interactive Narrative
  • Bert Cheung - Programming, Writing
  • Christine Elaine - Art, Writing
  • Aspen Miller - Art, Writing, SFX
My Roles: Gameplay Design, Programming, Narrative, Writing

Into the Paintscape is a visual novel created in the open-source Ren'Py engine. It was the result of a group term project for our Interactive Narrative class. Our story was inspired by the French Surrealism art style and it tells the story of Nia, a photographer who looks for her missing friend Selene who's trapped inside the worlds of her paintings.

Process

Since we were expected to provide a more serious narrative Christine and I looked at events in real-life to serve as an inspiration and foundation for our story. It was originally just myself and Christine as the team where I would function as the writer, programmer and gameplay designer while Christine would focus work on the art and writing.

Problem

Halfway through the term we added Aspen to our team. This was because we needed another person to help divide the workload and we also liked some of her artwork. Since she was working on a fantasy medieval narrative before we recruited her and Christine and I were not set on the story yet we had to overcome a writing block and choose between two contrasting tones/narratives. What kind of story, and what kind of setting did we want to tell? We didn't know yet.

Result

The three of us had brainstorming sessions to come to a decision on our narrative. We tried to merge the fantasy medieval setting of Aspen's narrative with the plot and story of someone searching for her friend, but ultimately we decided to stick with the modern day setting we had in mind. That also meant scrapping most, if not all of Aspen's narrative designs. It was a hard decision but we did it in order to have a stronger focus and not make it seem like a forced blending between two different genres and settings. It was also important to keep the scope of the project reasonable since having a fantasy setting would mean more sketching and brainstorming when it could be used for getting the project done. In the end adding Aspen was a great choice since the workload was split better and we were able to manage our time and sanity in the semester.

Some of the skills I've learned and used working on this project include:

  • Brainstorming and writing a narrative with branching choices and multiple endings
  • Using past programming and scripting experience to pick up Ren'Py within a semester
  • Using Ren'Py's triggers and scripting system to create dialogue and a visual novel experience including sounds, music, narrative choices and puzzles
  • Working with the team on art direction and integrating art assets
